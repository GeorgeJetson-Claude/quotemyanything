2028 Cruise Deals: Early Booking Tips to Save Hundreds (or Thousands)

Updated June 2026 · QuoteMyAnything Cruise Desk

2028 might seem far away, but smart cruisers are already locking in the best deals. With several new ships and expanded itineraries being announced, early birds are securing lower fares, better cabins, and exclusive perks.

Why 2028 Cruises Are Worth Booking Early

Cruise lines release schedules 18–24+ months in advance. Booking early for 2028 means:

  • Lowest available fares (prices typically rise 10-30% closer to sailing)
  • Prime cabin selection — especially oceanview, balcony, and suites
  • Flexible change policies on many lines
  • Access to launch promotions and new ship inaugural pricing

Current 2028 Highlights to Watch

  • Carnival Tropicale expected in 2028 (Excel class, likely Galveston homeport)
  • Additional Icon-class or Oasis-class deployments from Royal Caribbean
  • Expanded Celebrity, Princess, and Norwegian offerings in Europe, Asia, and Australia
  • More river cruise options and expedition sailings

Proven Strategies to Score the Best 2028 Deals

1. Book During "Wave Season" (January–March)

This is when lines release the biggest promotions. Watch for:

  • "Buy One Get One" or "Kids Sail Free"
  • Onboard credit offers
  • Reduced deposits (as low as $100–$200 per person)

2. Use Price Drop Protection

Most major lines allow you to reprice if the fare drops after booking (sometimes with a small fee). Book early, then monitor and adjust.

3. Consider These Money-Saving Tactics

  • Repositioning cruises: One-way transatlantic or Panama Canal sailings are often 30-50% cheaper per night.
  • Shoulder months: April, May, September, and October offer lower rates than peak summer or holidays.
  • Inside cabins or guarantee categories: Cheapest way to sail on new ships.
  • Group bookings or past guest discounts: Extra savings and perks.

4. Leverage Loyalty Programs

If you've sailed before, status benefits (free drinks, Wi-Fi, priority boarding, cabin upgrades) can add hundreds in value.

5. Watch for New Ship Inaugural Deals

New ships often have special pricing or extra perks for early sailings to build reviews and buzz. Carnival Festivale (2027) and its 2028 sister will likely have strong launch offers.

Best Places to Find 2028 Cruise Deals

  • Official cruise line websites (filter by 2028)
  • Cruise Critic, Vacations To Go, and Cruise.com for alerts
  • Travel agents specializing in cruises (often have unadvertised group rates)
  • Google Flights + cruise search tools for air + cruise packages

Realistic Savings Examples

Many cruisers report saving $500–$2,000+ per cabin by booking 12–18 months out vs. last minute. New ship sailings can sell out popular categories quickly, so early action pays off.

Final Advice

Don't wait for "perfect" pricing — the best deals go to those who act first. Set up fare alerts for your dream itinerary today, and consider a low-deposit booking you can adjust later.

2028 promises more ships, more destinations, and more reasons to sail. Start planning now and cruise smarter.
